Description

The goal is to present vaults in their simplest form, without opinions or assumptions about how or why they are implemented. Each vault will be described using the categories below, offering a neutral breakdown of its mechanics. After this baseline is established, bugs or flaws in specific protocols can be clearly identified by category…

Vault categorization

Shares Mechanism

How user deposits are represented and tracked via shares. This includes how shares are minted, burned, and valued over time.

Fees

All forms of fees charged by the vault — including performance, withdrawal, or management fees — and how they are applied and distributed.

Loss

How the vault handles scenarios where the underlying assets lose value. This includes mechanisms for accounting, absorbing, or passing on losses.

Yield

The method by which the vault generates returns on user deposits, including strategy execution, harvesting, and compounding processes.

IO (Input/Output)

How assets are deposited into and withdrawn from the vault. Covers processes, constraints (like limits or delays), and any asset conversion involved.

Vault State

Tracks the internal condition of the vault — such as paused states, caps, critical variables, and any state-dependent logic affecting functionality.
